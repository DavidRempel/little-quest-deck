Little Quest Deck

About this build
A stripped-back card-combo prototype. Fight one enemy at a time. Attack with selected cards, Discard X → Draw X to cycle cards, or Run from regular enemies. Cycling and failed runs let the enemy hit you for HP. Attacks refill your hand back toward your current hand size.

Rules
  • Attack with selected cards. The game automatically chooses the highest-scoring valid combo: Match, Sequence, or Flush. The Attack button turns green when the selected attack will defeat the enemy, red when it only deals partial damage.
  • Starting hand size is 5. Hand size can rise to a max of 7.
  • Score math: selected card count × size multiplier × combo-family bonuses. Match, Sequence, and Flush bonuses can stack. Matching the enemy weakness adds another bonus. Weapons add bonus damage to their attack family.
  • Combo bonuses: Match ×1.25, Sequence ×1.25, Flush ×1.5. A straight flush gets both Sequence and Flush. Match hands score in chunks, so two pair = pair + pair and full house = three-kind + pair.
  • Valid combos: Match = all selected cards are in matching-number groups, including pair, two pair, three/four of a kind, or full house. Sequence = 3+ consecutive numbers. Flush = 3+ same colour.
  • Enemy weakness is one broad family: Match, Sequence, or Flush. Combo bonuses stack; a straight flush gets both Sequence and Flush.
  • Enemies have HP. If your attack is less than their total HP, it still knocks their HP down, then they hit back.
  • Enemies 5, 10, and 15 are bosses. Enemy 16 is the final boss: -1 effective hand size, win prize on defeat, then you can keep playing endless mode.
  • Discard replaces selected cards. The enemy hits afterward, but only at half strength rounded down.
  • Run has a 50% chance to replace a regular enemy. If it fails, the enemy hits you. Bosses cannot be run from.
  • After an attack, played cards are replaced up to your current hand size.
  • Equipment slots start with basic no-bonus gear. New gear can be equipped or sold for gold. Weapon adds attack damage, Armor reduces damage, and Item gives utility bonuses.
  • Deck editing rewards — adding or removing cards — are not in this build yet.
