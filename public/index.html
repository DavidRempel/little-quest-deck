Little Quest Deck

About this build
A stripped-back card-combo prototype. Fight one enemy at a time. Attack with selected cards, Discard X → Draw X to cycle cards, or Run from regular enemies. Cycling and failed runs let the enemy hit you for HP. Attacks refill your hand back toward your current hand size.

Rules
  • Attack with selected cards. The game automatically chooses the highest-scoring valid combo: Match, Two Pair, Straight, Flush, or Straight Flush. The Attack button turns green when the selected attack will defeat the enemy, red when it only deals partial damage.
  • Starting hand size is 5. Future buffs can raise that hand size.
  • Score math: selected card count × combo-size multiplier. Weakness doubles the result. Straight Flush gets an extra ×2. Weapons add bonus damage to their attack type.
  • Multipliers: 2 cards ×2, 3 cards ×4, 4 cards ×8, 5 cards ×16.
  • Valid combos: Match = 2+ same number; Two Pair = exactly two pairs; Straight = 3+ consecutive numbers; Flush = 2+ same colour; Straight Flush = 3+ consecutive cards of one colour.
  • Straight Flush is powerful enough already, so enemies do not have Straight Flush as a weakness.
  • Enemies have HP. If your attack is less than their total HP, it still knocks their HP down, then they hit back.
  • Every 5th enemy is a boss. Defeating a boss always gives a visible reward. Some regular enemies also drop rewards.
  • Discard X → Draw X discards as many selected cards as you want, then draws the same number back, so your hand does not grow.
  • Run has a 50% chance to replace a regular enemy. If it fails, the enemy hits you. Bosses cannot be run from.
  • After an attack, played cards are replaced up to your current hand size.
  • Equipment slots: Weapon adds damage to an attack type, Armor reduces incoming damage, and Item gives a utility bonus such as max HP or all-attack damage.
  • Deck editing rewards — adding or removing cards — are not in this build yet.
